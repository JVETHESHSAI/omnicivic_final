— · A note from the maker

We built OmniCivic because every community I lived in had the same conversation three times a week: someone in the group chat reports a leak, someone else says it's been a month, the secretary asks who's handling it, the message scrolls away, nothing changes.

The point of this isn't more software. The point is that the thread doesn't disappear. A leak that's been open for six days looks like a leak that's been open for six days — visible to residents, to the admin, to the staff member it's assigned to. That visibility is the entire feature.

— · How it works

Three roles. One thread per issue.

Built for the way communities actually operate — not how they appear in pitch decks.

01

Residents report

They open the app, pick a category, drop a pin, optionally attach a photo, and submit. No chasing anyone. They can see exactly when it was acknowledged and who's handling it.

  • Photos & map pin on every complaint
  • Real-time status — Open → Assigned → In progress → Resolved
  • "Affected too" button so admins know what's burning
02

Admins route

Every new report lands in a triage queue. Admin assigns it to the right staff member, watches them work, reviews their proof when they say it's done.

  • Triage queue sorted by urgency and age
  • Auto-suggested staff based on category
  • Proof review gate before anything is marked resolved
03

Staff fix

Each staff member sees only their assigned tasks. They mark "started", do the work, submit proof (a photo + short note), and wait for admin to approve.

  • Personal queue — no inbox spam
  • Proof submission with photo
  • Rejection sends it back with reason — no silent failures
— · Why bother

You already use WhatsApp. So why this?

WhatsApp is great for "what's for dinner". It's not built for "the lift has been broken for nine days."

×

WhatsApp / paper logbooks

  • Messages scroll away. No one knows what's pending.
  • Who's responsible for this? Unclear, every time.
  • No proof a thing was actually fixed — just "OK done" texts.
  • New residents can't see history. They re-report old issues.
  • Heated arguments in the group chat about the same broken light for weeks.
✓

OmniCivic

  • Each issue has a stable URL, status, and assigned owner.
  • Residents see exactly who's working on it and when it started.
  • Photo proof required before something is marked resolved.
  • Full searchable history. New residents see what's already been handled.
  • The group chat goes back to being a group chat.
— · What happens after you sign up

Four steps. No salespeople.

  1. 1

    You submit a request

    Tell us your community name, your contact info, optionally a logo and your address (so we can pin your location on the map for residents).

  2. 2

    We review & approve

    Usually within a day. We'll generate a 4-character prefix unique to your community (e.g. MAR2 for Marina Mall) — it becomes part of every user's username.

  3. 3

    You get admin credentials

    You receive an email with your admin username and a temporary password. First sign-in forces you to set a new password.

  4. 4

    You invite residents & staff

    From the admin panel, create users one-by-one or in bulk. Each gets their own welcome email with their credentials. The platform is live.
